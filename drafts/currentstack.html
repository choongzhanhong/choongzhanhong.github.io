My current stationery stack05 Dec 2024

Through the high school to university, I have always preferred handwriting my notes. Nothing quite beats the tactile feel of pen being put to paper, and arguably, it boosts learning in the process.

Here's a breakdown of some of my stationery.

The MUJI pen is an all-timer when it comes to inexpensive, refillable, and simple, no-frills writing. I like my pens as fine as possible, so I appreciate the 0.38mm tip size. The ink comes out smoothly and are usually in brilliant shades (I simply enjoy the black and blue ones).

There's only so far you can get with a ballpoint pen, however. Ballpoints glide smoothly on paper, which is a feature I realised I don't need when I started using nib pens. The resistance and scratchiness you get from a nib just scratched the itch for me. You could feel the whole texture of the paper through the pen as an extension of your hand, a feeling that isn't altogether removed when using a ballpoint, but largely diminished, especially on thicker ballpoint tip sizes (which may be why I prefer finer pens).

I first began by using dip pens, before I got myself a fountain pen. The Pilot Kakuno was a game-changer for me. It's cheap and simple, all with a smile on its face. I got one with a converter, which allowed me to refill my ink instead of using cartridges, in the name of the environment. I use Pelikan 4001 Brilliant Schwarz, and it's nowhere near running out. As far as value-for-money goes, a fountain pen, converter, and ink pot beats everything by far.

As for my mechanical pencil, I used to own a Pilot Rexgrip that lasted me all my early schooling days, but it recently broke irreparably. I took to the bookstore to look for a replacement. I didn't just get the same pencil, nor any one on sale. This was a rare opportunity for a fresh start.

And finally, a toast to my pencil case, which has stuck with me for over a decade. I still recall purchasing this no-name case at my high school's bookstore, inscribing my name on its side with a permanent marker, not knowing just how long it would stay by my side. Its zipper works more than fine, and the fabric, stains aside (my fault), perfectly pristine.
