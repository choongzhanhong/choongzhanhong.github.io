Vinegar cures depression05 Dec 2024

A running joke at work attributed to me is the idea that "vinegar cures depression". If you were to look online, you'd see some studies on it, though the point of me writing this isn't to argue whether it does or not, or to what extent. Often, I would have some vinegar-rich foods for lunch, ranging from my homemade pickles to fishball noodles (one of my favourite staple Singaporean fare). Eating lunch in general makes me happy, but if it happens to contain vinegar, the remark that it "cures my depression" gets thrown around.

There is this humorous sentence you may see online, yeah I'll go ahead and integrate that into my belief system, which I think perfectly captures these inconsequential concepts. I don't actually believe it's the next big breakthrough in psychiatry, but I was going to eat vinegar-rich foods anyway (YEAH CHINESE FOOD!), so it'd be nicely convenient if it had that effect on my mental health in actuality.

A side effect of this joke may be me actually noticing when vinegar is a key ingredient in the food I eat, or maybe even actively seeking to incorporate it into my diet. I share a few condiments with my colleague, since most days we bring our own meal-prepped lunch which require a little more oomph in flavour. They are, so far, primarily relish and hot sauces, which are indeed vinegar-based! I now appreciate vinegar much more, for its flavour, depth, and lack of calories(!).

I wrote this at work, and at lunch I sat with my colleagues, the "awesome sauces" (as we called them) laid out in front of us. One of them bought mixed rice and remarked that the eggplant dish had already run out by lunchtime. Me and eggplants are like you and vinegar (or something to that effect), he said to me, to which I replied My eggplant is your eggplant (I like eggplants too!).

If you build it, they will come04 Dec 2024

I think I first read this (misattributed) quote from an achievement in Team Fortress 2, or perhaps somewhere else, but it know for a fact it wasn't from the movie, which I haven't seen anyway. Still, it stuck with me, perhaps in part due to its absurdity to me at the time (build what? who will come?).

Anyway, for a good part of my life this saying has sort of become a mantra to me to get me to initiate stuff, especially with friends. I had also been twiddling my thumbs about getting around to writing a blog for the longest time, but never being satisfied with how to do it. As such, I have some writings in Blogspot/Blogger, others in Medium, Tumblr... and now I'm back here on my own website in plain HTML, which isn't too bad. If I ever decide to uproot everything again, at least it's already all formatted for me.

So, I'm going to build it. I'll try to hold myself to writing at least something every week. This is me, enacting my mantra.
